ABOUT US

The West Vancouver Cricket Club, based at Hugo Ray Park in West Vancouver is one of the largest cricket clubs in Canada. It is rated the best cricketing facility in Western Canada.

It is our aim to develop a strong and successful club both on and off the field and to provide an enjoyable environment for all members, guests and spectators.

Our club field will field seven (7) competitive league teams playing in the British Columbia Mainland Cricket League (BCMCL) for the 2016 Season. The top teams play One Day International (ODI) while the rest of the teams play limited 45 overs. Games are played on Saturdays and Sundays. The regular season starts on the last weekend of April and runs to the Labour Day weekend. The club also participates in a Pre-Season Twenty20 Cricket Tournament which runs for 5 weekends prior to the start of the regular season.

Other cricket events/tournaments include:
  • Social Over 40's Cricket (mid week)
  • Junior Cricket - Under 12's and Under 17's programs
  • High peformance cricket programs
  • Summer Cricket Camp (July 4-8, 2016) at Hugo Ray Park
  • Women's Cricket (May to August)
  • The club supports and provides coaches for School Cricket
  • Social games during the season
  • Mid-week Twenty20 Cricket
  • 6-A-Side Tournaments
  • Matches against touring teams
  • Community and social cricket on Fridays from 6-8 pm (June - August)

Our club's home, Hugo Ray Park, has great facilities including 2 outdoor nets and 2 grounds - changing rooms with showers and ample free parking. The club is fortunate to have a clubhouse for use by members and guests. The clubhouse is located adjacent to the two cricket grounds and has numerous amenities including a licensed bar, a pool table and a ping pong table, kitchen, BBQ, and lounge and bar areas leading onto balconies which overlook the grounds. The clubhouse is also available for rental.

We are always looking for new members and if you and/or any of your friends are interested in playing cricket feel free to contact us.


CLUB HISTORY

The West Vancouver Cricket Club was formed in 1955 by three Kerrisdale players, Tom Guy, Dr George Mott and John Able, who felt that there was enough potential in the area to form a club. That summer a series of friendly fixtures was played against other clubs in the mainland league, using a small nucleus and borrowed players. The response was so favourable that in 1956 a full 'A' team was entered into the league. In 1957, a team was entered in both 'A' and 'B; Divisions and in the early 1960's the club fielded three teams for a number of years. However this was later cut back to two teams. Any West Vancouver player will point out that the lack of championship points up the rather holiday air that West Vancouver brings to its cricket!

In it's infancy, the ground itself could be found in Ambleside, 100 yards from the beach with the scenic North Shore Mountains rising almost from the edge of the ground. As far as beauty is concerned, few grounds can compare. The new ground now as it stands at Hugo Ray Park is a little higher into the mountains which has become the permanent home of the West Vancouver Cricket Club with full playing, practicing and social facilities. There is no truth to the old story that the submarine ball was first discovered at West Vancouver's Ambleside pitch during high tide.

West Vancouver boasts one of the largest junior programs in BC and every year the number of teams increase. Teams play in a Saturday Morning Junior League and there is an active schools program in conjunction with schools on the North Shore.
The future looks bright for West Vancouver, as the teams are gaining every year in both numbers and strength.

Life Members

The West Vancouver Cricket Club has a long and proud history dating way back to 1955. Last Saturday, at their clubhouse in West Vancouver's Hugo Ray Park, the club honoured its lifetime members and alumni in a special ceremony acknowledging their dedication and commitment to the sport. Currently, the club is at risk of losing their playing fields at Hugo Ray and are fighting to preserve the grass fields - fields they say are among the best in North America. The club has produced champion league players at both the local and national levels.

PROGRAMS

JUNIOR

West Vancouver Cricket Club has the largest junior cricket program in Vancouver with three teams playing. Over 40 young players from across Metro Vancouver are enrolled in our program and play in the spring / summer season (May to July).

The main aim of the club is to provide healthy recreation in an enjoyable, optimistic environment while playing a sport! With the help of our junior coaches, senior cricketers and hopefully some enthusiastic parents, we are confident of another outstanding year ahead.

FACILITY

Located in West Vancouver with views of the scenic North Shore mountains, Hugo Ray Park is home to the West Vancouver Cricket Club and their clubhouse. The fields are used primarily for cricket in the summer with soccer being played in the winter months.

The Pavilion is available for public rentals. Groups can host seminars and it is the perfect alternative for corporate meetings. Our downstairs board room can accommodate up to 25 people for a meeting or training session.
